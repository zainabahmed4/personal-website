Student Onboarding Site

UX Research Wireframing HTML CSS MkDocs

The Challenge

New student hires at Argonne were struggling to navigate the onboarding process due to scattered and unclear documentation.

UX Research

  • User Interviews: Conducted 1-on-1 interviews with former student hires to understand their frustrations and navigation patterns during onboarding.
  • Diary Studies: Asked new hires to document their onboarding experiences over a few days, revealing step-by-step pain points and emotional responses.
  • Surveys: Collected broader quantitative feedback to identify common barriers and confusion points in the existing documentation.
  • Pain Point Synthesis: Mapped and categorized issues across users to inform design decisions and prioritize fixes based on frequency and impact.

Paula Brown

New Student Hire

Who is Paula?

Paula is a graduate student starting her first research position at Argonne. She's excited but overwhelmed by the amount of information she needs to process.

Goals

Quickly understand her role, access necessary systems, and feel confident in her new environment.

Onboarding Experience

Received multiple emails with conflicting information, struggled to find the right documentation, and felt lost navigating different platforms.

Pain Points & Challenges

Information overload, unclear communication, difficulty accessing systems, lack of centralized resources.

The Process

Mapped user journeys and created personas to understand diverse student needs. Collaborated with my team to write and structure all page content, ensuring clarity and inclusivity across student appointment types. Designed low-to-high fidelity wireframes, then built the site using MkDocs with a clear and accessible layout.

Recorded step-by-step video tutorials to guide new hires through the onboarding process. Ensured visual consistency by aligning the site with Argonne’s internal branding standards and design system.

Outcome

Final design helped clarify the onboarding process and will be used for future student cohorts. Stakeholder and user feedback highlighted improved accessibility and usability.
